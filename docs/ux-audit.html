otium — competitive UX audit

Readers surveyed: NetNewsWire · new Reeder · Feedbin · Feedly · Inoreader · Readwise Reader · Matter · Unread · Artifact (RIP). Filtered for otium's intentional-consumption thesis, the e-ink / Boox Palma 2 constraint, and the explicit-signals-only stance. July 2026.

otium isn't missing much that fits its thesis. The two things it is missing — full-text in the reader (#58/#52) and a saved view (#57) — are really about finishing affordances the UI already shows the user. Rank those first. The genuinely net-new, high-leverage bets are all e-ink/Palma-specific. And a surprising amount of what the big engagement readers do, otium should keep refusing out loud.

Two external proof points

The new Reeder killed unread counts — a respected mainstream RSS developer independently concluded unread-count anxiety is the thing to remove, and shipped a reader with "no concept of read/unread." otium is on the leading edge of a real movement, not out on a limb — and it goes further, with finite sessions and an explainable ranker rather than a still-infinite (if guilt-free) timeline. Corollary warning: Reeder's most-requested missing feature is swipe-to-advance inside the reader — otium already solved that with scroll-snap. Don't regress it.

Artifact died of scope creep. Instagram's founders built an algorithmic engagement feed, bolted on a social/link-posting layer to chase growth, and shut it down. The engagement-feed space is a graveyard, and adding social surface area is what pulled Artifact off its own thesis. otium's edge is the opposite stance.

Ideas worth stealing (thesis-fit order)

  1. Session TTS with read↔listen handoff (Matter). The single most on-thesis steal: a paced, finite session is exactly what you'd want to listen to on a walk. Eyes-free intentional consumption, zero engagement mechanics. (#62)
  2. e-ink mode + volume-key advance (Readwise Reader). Physical-button page turns are the most-loved e-ink affordance; touch on an e-ink digitizer is laggy. (#60)
  3. Pinch-to-collapse + save-without-open gestures (Unread). Gesture grammar in service of less reading — explicit-signal triage.
  4. Intent presets (NetNewsWire smart feeds). "Morning 10-min," "Sunday long-read" as one-tap saved intents — reinforces ritual.
  5. "Since you last sat down" freshness boundary (Reeder's position model). Answers "am I caught up?" without ever showing a count.
  6. Inline Lookup (Readwise). Select text → inline explanation; deepens the chosen item instead of pulling toward the next. Gate behind an LLM call.

Deliberately not stealing: AI auto-summaries, dedupe, gamified inbox-zero triage, rules engines. All optimize throughput — the opposite of the thesis.

Prioritized roadmap

  1. Full-text in reader — #58 render content:encoded (fixes 7 feeds instantly) then #52 extract the ~3 partial ones.
  2. Saved / collections view — #57 (completes the Save button).
  3. Offline caching of session content — #59 (Palma-specific; depends on full text).
  4. e-ink mode + volume-key advance — #60.
  5. Reader typography controls — #61.
  6. Session TTS / listen-mode — #62.
  7. Share + copy link — #56. · Scoped search — #63.
  8. Intent presets · collapse/save gestures · inline lookup · cross-device sync · desktop keyboard nav.

What to refuse — out loud

The most useful output of this audit is the list of "table stakes" otium should proudly not have. Naming them is part of the product's identity:

  • Unread counts, badges, "mark all read."
  • Infinite / algorithmic "For You" feed.
  • AI auto-summaries, dedupe, gamified catch-up triage.
  • Rules/automation engines and per-item tags.
  • In-app social / commentary / link-posting (share-out is a link, not a network).
  • Engagement surveillance — dwell time, implicit interest scoring. Signals stay explicit (Open/Like/Save/Skip).

e-ink, stated plainly

  • Motion is the enemy. Scroll-snap (one discrete transition) is fine; no continuous animation, fades, spinners, skeleton shimmers. Loading = instant swaps or static text.
  • Use the hardware buttons. Volume-key session advance beats touch on a laggy digitizer.
  • Contrast over color. The "why this" breakdown, feed icons, and weight buckets must read in pure grayscale at high contrast — test at ~1-bit, not just desaturated.
Bottom line: the risk isn't under-featuring — it's getting talked into the features that killed the incumbents. Rank the two "finish the promise" gaps first (#58, #57); the differentiated bets are all e-ink/Palma-specific plus session TTS.
