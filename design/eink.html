E-Ink, in motion

You picked the e-ink language. Now the question is how it moves. Nine variations, same look, exploring three things: how you travel through content, how you like / open / preview, and how each kind of media renders in grayscale.

← all 18 directions the base e-ink app experience guidelines

All nine share the exact same skin - paper, Charter serif, hard hairlines, dithered media, and a deliberately slow e-ink "refresh" flash on every move. Only the interaction and media treatment differ, so you're judging how it feels in the hand, not how it looks. Every preview is live; tap it to open full-screen and actually swipe, hold, and stamp.

How to read this. These are interaction sketches - some gestures are more finished than others. Feel for the one-at-a-time rhythm (no blur-past), and whether like/open/preview feel natural under your thumb. You can mix: "the Swipe Deck's gestures with the Media Reel's previews." The media treatments in #9 are the reference set regardless of which navigation wins.
